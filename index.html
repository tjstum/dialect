      The colors show the estimated probability that a randomly chosen person in each area would answer a randomly chosen question the same way you did — the method Josh Katz used for the 2013 New York Times dialect quiz. This version is built on hand-modeled regional probabilities grounded in the Harvard Dialect Survey (Vaux & Golder), the Cambridge Online Survey of World Englishes, and the Atlas of North American English. It is an educational reproduction of the method, not the NYT's proprietary dataset. Map geometry: us-atlas. Dialect-survey data is © its authors under CC BY-NC-SA 3.0.
